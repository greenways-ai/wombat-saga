# The Kangaroo Mob (The Friction Engines)

**"Gravity is just an opinion. Kicking it in the face is a fact."**

The **Kangaroo Mob** represents the Sixth Thread (**The Work/Practice**). They are the athletes, the warriors, and the kinetic engines of Greater Arnam.

## The Philosophy of the Bounce

The Kangaroos believe that **Resistance is Fuel**. They worship "The Bunda" (The Great Leap)â€”the act of generating upward momentum by pushing hard against the earth.

*   **The Friction Engine:** They do not seek an easy life. They seek "Good Friction." A path with no obstacles is a path with no growth.
*   **The Tailwind:** They believe that if you move fast enough, you create your own weather. A stationary animal is a victim of the climate; a moving Kangaroo *is* the storm.

## The Practice: The Boxing

The Kangaroo practice is physical and combative.

*   **The Spar:** Young bucks are taught to spar not to hurt each other, but to "toughen the hide." They trade blows to locate their own weak points. A bruise is just the body's way of marking a place that needs more armor.
*   **The Static Hold:** Paradoxically, their hardest practice is "The Statue." They stand perfectly still for hours, muscles vibrating with potential energy, learning to contain the explosion until the exact millisecond it is needed.

## Role in Greater Arnam

The Kangaroo Mob serves as the **Deep-Work Artisans** and **Shock Troops**.

*   **The Churners:** Their heavy tails and massive feet churn the topsoil, breaking up the hard crust so the Wombats can dig deeper and plants can take root. They wake up the earth.
*   **The Boundary Keepers:** They patrol the edges of the territory. Their size and speed make them the "Visible Deterrent" against encroaching chaos.
*   **The Momentum:** When the community stagnates, the Kangaroos arrive to "kick things up." They bring an injection of high-velocity energy that forces everyone else to wake up and move.

> **"You want to get over that fence? Don't look at the top rail. Look at the ground. Kick the earth hard enough, and it will throw you over. Usage is payment."**
