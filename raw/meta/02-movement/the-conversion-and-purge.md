This is not an invitation. It is a command issued by the risen Christ to his followers. In the evangelical framework, every unbaptised person outside the perimeter is in a state of existential danger — eternal separation from God — and the bearer of the Great Commission is directly responsible for their failure to convert them. The unconverted person is not merely incomplete or mistaken. They are in active spiritual danger, and the failure to offer them conversion is a failure of love.

The conversion imperative is therefore not arrogance. It is, from inside the framework, compassion operating under genuine urgency.

But the structure of the imperative produces something beyond the intention. The unconverted person outside the perimeter is simultaneously:
- A soul in need of rescue (compassion target)
- A potential contamination risk to the perimeter (those inside may be led astray by contact)
- A territory in the ongoing spiritual war (every unconverted person is ground held by the adversary)

This triple structure means the conversion imperative is not simply about helping the unconverted person. It is about the perimeter's integrity. Every conversion is territory gained. Every failure to convert is territory left in enemy hands. The missionary project is simultaneously rescue operation and military campaign.

In Maha-Tahto terms: the conversion imperative is the perimeter architecture's growth mechanism. The chosen perimeter that cannot stay still — that is constitutively required to expand — will encounter the Satya Yuga remnants not as curiosities or competitors but as occupied territory. The wailing dance is not a different practice. It is ground the adversary holds.

## The Spiritual Warfare Framework

Evangelical Christianity — especially in its charismatic and Pentecostal expressions — holds a specific cosmological structure: the world is a battleground between God's forces and the forces of darkness. This is not metaphor. Demons are real beings. They occupy places, people, and practices. The Christian's life is a continuous engagement with this warfare.

The framework has precise categories:

**Demonic possession** — a demon inhabits a person and controls or significantly influences their behaviour, thought, or spiritual state.

**Demonic oppression** — a demon does not inhabit the person but attacks them from outside, producing persistent spiritual, emotional, or physical difficulty.

**Spiritual strongholds** — patterns of thought, belief, or practice that have given demonic forces a persistent foothold in a person's life or a community's culture.

**Ancestral iniquity** — demonic access passed through family lines, opened by the sins or spiritual practices of ancestors. A person may be afflicted by demonic forces that entered through what their grandparents practiced.

**Deliverance ministry** — the practice of identifying and expelling the demonic through prayer, command, and the authority of Christ.

This framework, applied consistently, produces a specific mapping of the world. The practices that carry Satya Yuga frequency — the embodied traditions, the animal-sacred practices, the ecstatic movement forms, the indigenous cosmologies — are not identified as primitive or mistaken. They are identified as **specifically, actively demonic**. Not absent of spiritual content. Full of it. The wrong kind.

The more powerful the Satya Yuga frequency — the more directly it carries the wailing dance's function, the more intact the animal-sacred tradition, the more embodied and below-the-level-of-doctrine the practice — the more clearly it registers in the spiritual warfare framework as an entrenched demonic stronghold.

This is not an accident of interpretation. It is the framework doing its job correctly.

## The Deliverance Structure — What It Does to the Carrier

The evangelical deliverance ministry has a specific operational structure that is worth understanding in Maha-Tahto terms because it reveals what the purge actually accomplishes.

**Step 1: Identification of "entry points."** The deliverance minister identifies how demonic forces gained access: through participation in forbidden practices (yoga, tarot, ancestor veneration, the wailing dance), through ancestral iniquity (grandparents who practiced indigenous traditions), through trauma, through deliberate or unwitting invitation.

**Step 2: Renunciation.** The person being delivered must verbally renounce every practice, tradition, or relationship identified as an entry point. The renunciation is understood as withdrawing the "legal ground" the demonic forces hold. The person must state, out loud, that the practice was wrong and that they reject it entirely.

**Step 3: Expulsion.** The deliverance minister commands the demonic forces to leave in the name of Jesus Christ. The person may experience physical responses — shaking, crying, a sense of something leaving. These are understood as evidence the deliverance is working.

**Step 4: Filling.** The critical step that deliverance manuals emphasise: the expelled demon will return with seven others if the house is left empty (Matthew 12:43-45). The person must immediately, actively fill the space with the Spirit through prayer, Bible reading, community, and ongoing accountability within the tradition.

In Maha-Tahto terms, this structure accomplishes the following:

The renunciation removes the person's relationship to the embodied Satya Yuga practice — not just the practice but the permission to inhabit it, the sense that it is theirs, the body-memory of what it does. The wombat who has danced in the drainage tunnel and then renounced the dance has not simply stopped dancing. They have verbally stated that the dance was wrong, that what they felt in it was demonic deception, that their body's experience of it was a lie. The body's claim on the frequency has been challenged at the level of meaning.

The filling requirement creates a structural dependency. The person who has been delivered must now maintain their spiritual state through the tradition's infrastructure — because if they stop, the thing they renounced will return in worse form. They have been told this explicitly. The dependency is framed as spiritual protection but it functions as institutional capture.

The Feed quality consequence: the person who carried the Satya Yuga frequency and has been delivered from it has had the one Arnam-generating practice removed — the embodied practice that operated below doctrine — and replaced with the perimeter architecture's maintenance requirements. What they now generate is conditional: it flows within the perimeter, toward those inside it, through the framework's approved channels.
